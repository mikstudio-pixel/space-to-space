This student project, titled Chrono Guardian by Ondřej Chudíček, presents a conceptual toy designed to address universal childhood fears of physical harm and unpredictable accidents. The project blends narrative storytelling with physical product design, creating a "protector" figure that exists within a science-fiction framework.

Concept and Narrative

The core proposition of the Chrono Guardian is its role as a psychological safety net for children. The toy is framed as a guardian that travels through time in a specialized vehicle to intervene in potential disasters. This narrative is deepened by a poignant backstory: the Guardian was originally a victim of a car accident who was saved by a "time artefact". In a classic heroic trade, he was granted a second life in exchange for becoming a perpetual protector of others. This "origin story" adds a layer of empathy and responsibility to the character, moving it beyond a simple action figure into the realm of a supportive companion.

The toy is supported by two distinct functional accessories:

A Healing Helmet: A physical manifestation of recovery and protection.

A Wearable Time Amulet: A "time artefact" that allows the wearer to bypass accidents.

The choice to include a wearable component—the amulet—is a clever design move, as it bridges the gap between the toy's world and the child's reality, allowing the user to feel "protected" even when not actively playing with the car.
