The Martin - The Postman Robot project, designed by Nuño Navarro Hernández for the PV268 Digital Design course, presents a robotic companion created to assist children with anxieties related to the domestic environment. The project focuses on helping users navigate "scary" parts of the home and complete intimidating daily tasks.
Character Identity and Background

The central character is M4RTn (also referred to as Martin or M-4 RoboTron), a specialized "mail robot". While his primary identity is a postman, his role in the child's life is that of a mechanical helper. The name evolved through several iterations, including "RoboTron" and "M4," before settling on the more personable "Martin".

Design and "Weapons" for Safety

To address childhood fears, Martin is equipped with specific mechanical features that the designer refers to as "weapons," which are actually tools for exploration and assistance:

Mechanical Hand: A large, silver-colored articulated limb that allows the robot to interact with objects in potentially "scary" or hard-to-reach areas of the house.

Night Vision Goggles: Specialized eyewear designed to help the robot (and by extension, the child) see into dark corners, effectively neutralizing the fear of the unknown in poorly lit rooms.

The physical model is a small, humanoid figure with a metallic head and a blue torso, often photographed alongside electronic components like circuit boards to emphasize his robotic nature.

Packaging and Visuals

The project's packaging stays true to the character's profession as a postman. The boxes are designed to look like traditional airmail packages, complete with red and blue striped borders and a space for the owner's name ("This M. RoboTron belongs to...").

The visual documentation includes sketches of the robot's facial expressions and goggles, showing a focus on creating a character that is both technologically advanced and emotionally approachable. By turning mundane chores and scary household spots into a collaborative mission with a "mail robot," the project recontextualizes domestic anxiety as a shared adventure in home maintenance.
